NARESH BHATTA

Naresh Bhatta is a registered Civil Engineer in Nepal and researcher working at the intersection of experimental work, advanced materials, and structural member testing, including destructive and non-destructive testing (DT/NDT), finite element modeling (FEM), and machine learning. His work spans UHPC, geopolymer binders, and waste-valorized materials—from mix design and laboratory testing to microstructural characterization and durability assessment.

On the computational side, he develops interpretable machine learning models for structural performance prediction using SHAP and PDP analyses, alongside nonlinear FEM modeling in Abaqus and structural design using SAP2000, ETABS, and SAFE.

He has authored and co-authored multiple peer-reviewed publications in international Scopus-indexed journals, with additional work currently under review.

Currently, he is working as a Project Engineer at Gham Power Nepal, where he designs RCC and steel structures for energy infrastructure, while actively pursuing research in sustainable construction materials and data-driven structural engineering.

He is open to graduate opportunities, research collaborations, and technically demanding roles.
